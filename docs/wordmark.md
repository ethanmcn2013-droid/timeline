# Wordmark direction — Roadmap
## 2026-05-08 · Cycle 2 · creative-director call

Three directions explored. Direction 1 chosen and implemented as
RoadmapMark in src/components/brand/wordmark.tsx.

## Direction 1 — Hairline route (CHOSEN)
A 32-pixel horizontal hairline with a small filled dot sliding along
it. The mark IS a road. Dot animates left-to-right on hover or
scroll-into-view. In static contexts (OG cards, favicon) it freezes
mid-line.
**Why chosen:** strongest semantic load — actually says "roadmap"
without saying it. Distinct from Tasks's "tasks·" while sharing the
dot-as-status motif (sister-brand DNA without copying). Motion story
maps directly to the cinematic demo (the dot moves as the user
"travels" the roadmap).

## Direction 2 — Forward chevron
Two hairlines forming a tight ›. Could pulse subtly on its leading
edge. Implies "what's coming" through directional vector.
**Why not chosen:** chevrons are common (Vercel, Linear used
variants); finding fresh-feeling negative space is the design problem.
Less semantically rich than the route.

## Direction 3 — Sequence of three dots
· · · where the rightmost is filled (shipped), middle is half-filled
(doing), leftmost is hollow (next). Encodes the status vocabulary in
the mark itself.
**Why not chosen:** reads as "more text coming" or "loading" before it
reads as "roadmap." Over-clever.

## Revisit cadence
If Direction 1 doesn't survive contact with the cinematic demo
(Deliverable 6), reconsider Direction 2 as a fallback.
