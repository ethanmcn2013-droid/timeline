## Direction

Editorial Plan Room makes purpose, movement, and human judgement first-class. It is designed for a public reader or collaborator who needs to understand the plan without prior product context.

The option reads like an edited briefing, not a database and not a marketing page. It gives the current direction and its supporting reason more space while retaining the canonical ladder as the complete receipt.

## Structural signature

- The owner view is an asymmetric working document with a change and decision margin.
- The public view gives one Now item lead treatment, followed by Soon and Later indexes and closing Done and Refused records.
- The shared update is a forwardable article led by current direction and `What changed, and why`.
- Item detail separates narrative meaning from a compact facts column, then restores surrounding plan context.
- Datelines, section numbering, and measured line lengths create editorial rhythm without display-scale theatre.

The option remains distinct in grayscale because hierarchy comes from composition, scale, and margin placement.

## Surface decisions

| Surface | Phase 1 composition | Intended result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Owner plan | Plan chapters, selected-row tools, owner-only note, and change or decision margin. | Edit public wording alongside the judgement that supports it. |
| Public timeline | Lead Now item, Soon and Later index, then settled records. | Purpose and current direction are understood before the full inventory. |
| Shared update | Standfirst, lead direction, paired change and reason records, attention summary, and full ladder. | Strongest forwardable reading without invented metrics. |
| Item detail | Narrative meaning and next step beside public facts, history, and plan context. | Explain why the item is here and what follows. |

Owner notes remain owner-only. Public, update, and detail consume the same whitelisted projection used by the other options.

## Owner interaction position

The editorial margin is contextual, not another source of truth. Selection reveals the common owner tools beside the public wording and latest change. Publication controls keep working preview, published view, last publication, and unpublished count explicit.

The lab proves the proposed editorial relationship around owner operations. It does not imply a collaborative editor, comments, automatic narrative generation, or a production publishing workflow.

## Strengths under review

- Strong ten-second articulation of purpose and current direction.
- Strongest candidate for a shared update that survives forwarding.
- Movement reasons and refusals receive appropriate narrative weight.
- Confidence and timing read as plain language rather than scores.
- Current direction remains distinct from the full-plan receipt.

## Tradeoffs and required scrutiny

- More vertical space can slow frequent owner curation.
- A lead item can imply false priority if order is not maintained deliberately.
- Dense data needs line-length and repetition controls without losing facts.
- Editorial labels must not drift into marketing language.
- Narrative, attention, and full-ladder sections must each answer a different question.

## Phase 2 boundary

Selection would approve the editorial hierarchy, not an invented authoring or publication system. Production implementation would still require canonical data mapping, a strict server projection, durable publication snapshots, anonymous-share proof, source-parameter preservation, link-preview and print verification, and decision-complete migration of existing histories.

No production route, database record, publish action, or public URL is changed by this Phase 1 option.
